QUESTION (i)

It is clearly visible in FIGURE 1, while plotting the specific dates against the total cases in ggplot we see an upsurge in the total number of cases of COVID from 15th March onwards, having recorded a total of 114 cases on that particular day, rising upto 987 total cases as of 28th March 2020 in India.

ggplot(time_series,aes(x=Date,y=`Daily New Cases`))+geom_point(colour="darkgreen")+labs(title = "FIGURE 2: DAILY NEW CASES OF COVID")

In the above Figure 2, we see date plotted against daily new cases, which indictes the visibility range of the new cases that are recorded daily. There has been increase in the daily new cases of COVID from 14th March, and if observed on the number scale taking duration from 14th March to 28th March, the daily new cases lie strictly between 18 to 160 in number with certain fluctuations in between .

ggplot(time_series,aes(x=Date,y=`Recovery`))+geom_point(colour="red")+labs(title = "FIGURE 3: THE RECOVERY RATES FROM COVID")

In the above FIGURE 3 , we see the recovery rates in India aren’t very high. On 9th March, out of the total 47 cases of COVID existing in India, the recovery rate is just 1, but on the 13th March the recovery rate jumped to a number of 6, with certain fluctuations in numbers likewise, followed by an increase in the recovery rate to 10 as of 23rd March.The highest recovery rate was on 27th March, a total of 28 patients recovered from COVID on that particular day.

QUESTION (ii)

India having a high population density,is more susceptible to COVID so therefore the lock-down measures as a means of keeping social distancing is effective to check the spread of corona, as it will reduce contact between the people,eventually preventing the transmission of the contagious disease from one person to the other. Also during this period of lockdown, it becomes easier to track the people infected,impose quarantines and reduce fatalities. Extensive measures to find, isolate,test,treat and trace seems to be the best option to control COVID in India.
